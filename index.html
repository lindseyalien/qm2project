Natural Disasters: Evolution, Impact and Response

Abstract

Our project asks the question of whether the intensity of natural disasters is linked to the intensity of the popular reaction to these disasters. Specifically, we investigated five well-documented disaster types - earthquakes, volcanoes, storms, wildfires, and floods - comparing disaster data from the EM-DAT database (ref) and media data from Google Trends. This data was presented on world maps and scatter plots, to build an image of potential links between the two factors. Our research shows that the link between disaster size and reaction size is very weak, and in some cases cannot be seen at all. This supports the literature, which suggests a multitude of other factors which may lead to a large or small media response: for example, geographical location, socioeconomics and risk perception. Why is this important, what have we shown that might help people?
